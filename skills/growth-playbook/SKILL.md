# Growth Playbook

You have some traction data. Now figure out what actually works and do more of it.

## What You Do

1. Read METRICS-REPORT.md to understand current acquisition channels and CAC
2. Read LEAN-CANVAS.md to understand initial growth assumptions
3. Analyze each acquisition channel:
   - How many users came from it?
   - What was the CAC?
   - What's the retention quality (do they stay)?
   - Can you scale it? (Ceiling: Product Hunt is one-time; paid ads can scale indefinitely)
4. Rank channels by CAC and retention quality
5. Identify your "golden channel" — highest quality users, sustainable CAC
6. Kill or deprioritize channels that don't work
7. Experiment with 1-2 new channels aligned to your best customer profile
8. Avoid spray-and-pray: Pick a few channels and go deep, not many shallow

## The Golden Channel

What's your best acquisition source?

```
Criteria:
1. Lowest CAC among repeatable channels
2. Highest retention (users who actually stick around)
3. Scalability (can you do more of it?)
4. Alignment (users match your ideal customer profile)

Example:
- Channel: Twitter organic + word-of-mouth
- CAC: $0 (founder's network)
- D7 retention: 65%
- Scalability: Limited (requires founder time)
- Better move: Invest in making product so good people recommend it (improve retention first)

Alternative:
- Channel: Google Ads
- CAC: $18
- D7 retention: 35%
- Scalability: Very high (unlimited budget available)
- Better move: A/B test ad copy, landing pages to improve conversion; improve product to improve retention

Another:
- Channel: Strategic partnerships
- CAC: $0-5 (partner splits cost)
- D7 retention: 50%
- Scalability: High (many potential partners)
- Better move: Double down — identify 10 partner targets, get 2-3 live this quarter
```

```
## What NOT to Do

**No spray and pray.** Don't:
- Run ads to every platform simultaneously (dilutes learning)
- Copy what competitors are doing without understanding your own data
- Hire growth person before you have repeatable channel (premature)
- Spend on vanity metrics (Twitter followers, newsletter opens) that don't translate to retention

## Red Flags to Avoid

1. **"Our CAC is only $X because we got early adopters from [free channel]."**
   - Reality: Once you move to paid channels, costs rise. Paid CAC is the real benchmark.

2. **"We'll do content marketing and go viral."**
   - Reality: Content is slow. With 2-3 month runway, you need immediate channels. Content is for month 6+.

3. **"Let's do paid ads, it scales infinitely."**
   - Reality: Only if your product converts and retains. Bad product + paid ads = expensive fail.

4. **"We'll hire a growth person to fix this."**
   - Reality: Growth person optimizes. If you don't have a working channel to optimize, they're useless.

## Monthly Review Checklist

Run this every month:

- [ ] Did channels perform as expected?
- [ ] Did any channel's CAC change? (More expensive = less attractive)
- [ ] Did retention stay consistent?
- [ ] Did new experiment show promise or kill it?
- [ ] Are we doubling down on golden channel or diversifying too much?
- [ ] What's the next channel to experiment with?

Remember: Growth is a ladder. Start with one working channel. Master it. Then add the next.
```

Key mindset: You don't need big marketing budgets or viral luck. You need one channel that works, with economics that make sense, that you can do repeatedly. Find it. Master it. Then scale it.
