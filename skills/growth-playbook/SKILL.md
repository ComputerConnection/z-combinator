# Growth Playbook

You have some traction data. Now figure out what actually works and do more of it.

---

## Phase 0: Grounding and Prerequisites

**Purpose:** Understand current state and prepare for channel analysis

**Exit criteria:** You have METRICS-REPORT.md and LEAN-CANVAS.md accessible, and you understand your current acquisition channels

### What This Skill Does

You're going to take your real data and identify your "golden channel" — the acquisition source that actually works (good CAC, strong retention, scalable). Then you'll stop wasting time on channels that don't work and experiment with 1-2 new ones. No spray and pray.

**Key principle:** You don't need big marketing budgets or viral luck. You need one channel that works, with economics that make sense, that you can do repeatedly. Find it. Master it. Then scale it.

## Phase 1: Channel Analysis Setup (One Question at a Time)

### Question 1A: Current Acquisition Channels Inventory

**Re-ground:** We're building your growth playbook. Phase 1 is analyzing each acquisition channel you used to get customers.

**Simplify:** List every way customers found you. Product Hunt? Twitter? Paid ads? Friends? For each channel, we'll measure: how many users, how much spend, what was retention quality, can you scale it?

**Recommend:** Can you list your acquisition channels with user counts and spend?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I have clear data on all channels: users acquired, spend, retention by channel
B) I have rough estimates for channels and some spend data
C) I tracked some channels but not comprehensively
D) I know roughly where users came from but no hard data

**If A:** "Perfect. We'll deep-dive into each one."

**If D:** "Let's rebuild this. Which channel brought the most users? Which brought the highest quality (best retention)?"

---

### Question 1B: CAC by Channel

**Re-ground:** Channels identified. Now: cost per customer.

**Simplify:** For each channel, divide total spend by customers acquired. That's CAC. Paid ads might be $30/customer. Twitter organic might be $0. Product Hunt is free but one-time.

**Recommend:** Can you calculate CAC for each channel?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Yes, I have CAC for each channel
B) I can estimate for some but not all
C) I only have blended CAC (all channels together)
D) No spend — all organic channels

---

### Question 1C: Retention Quality by Channel

**Re-ground:** CAC captured. Now: do the customers stick around?

**Simplify:** This is critical. Users from one channel might have 50% D7 retention. Users from another channel might have 10%. That changes everything about which channel to scale.

**Recommend:** Can you measure Day 7 or Day 30 retention by acquisition channel?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Yes, I have retention metrics by channel
B) I can do a rough split (organic users vs paid users)
C) I can measure retention overall but not by channel
D) I haven't looked at this yet

**If D:** "This is essential. If you don't know which channels give you the stickiest users, you're optimizing for the wrong thing. Can you pull this data? Tag each user by source and see who comes back."

## Phase 2: Deep Channel Scoring

### Question 2A: Ranking Channels by Efficiency

**Re-ground:** We have channel data. Phase 2 is scoring each channel.

**Simplify:** We'll rank channels by (CAC + Retention Quality + Scalability). A $10 CAC channel with 40% D7 retention that you can do indefinitely beats a free channel with 5% retention that only works once.

**Recommend:** Which channel looks best on paper?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) One channel stands out as best (good CAC + strong retention + scalable)
B) A few channels are decent but none are obviously superior
C) It's all mixed — hard to call a winner
D) All channels are terrible; nothing is working

**If D:** "That's useful information. Before we experiment with new channels, let's figure out why these aren't working. Is it product (people don't stay?) or marketing (wrong audience?)"

---

### Question 2B: One-Time vs Repeatable Channels

**Re-ground:** Channels scored. Now: which can you repeat?

**Simplify:** Product Hunt is one-time (you can't launch twice). Twitter organic is repeatable (you can keep tweeting). Paid ads are indefinitely scalable (unlimited budget). Understand the ceiling.

**Recommend:** For your best channels, are they repeatable or one-time?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) My best channel is repeatable/scalable (can do it again next month)
B) My best channels are one-time events (Product Hunt, press, etc.)
C) Mix of both
D) I'm not sure about the difference

**If B:** "Then you need a repeatable channel. One-time channels only work for launch. What can you do NEXT month to keep growing?"

---

### Question 2C: The Golden Channel Decision

**Re-ground:** Scalability analyzed. Now: identify your golden channel.

**Simplify:** The golden channel is: lowest CAC among REPEATABLE channels + highest retention + aligned with your ideal customer profile. This is where you focus 80% of effort.

**Recommend:** Which channel should be your golden channel (the one you'll scale aggressively)?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Clear golden channel identified (e.g., Twitter organic + word-of-mouth)
B) A few channels competing for top spot
C) No clear winner; all channels are mediocre
D) Haven't figured this out yet

**After user chooses, ask:** How will you scale this channel? Be specific. "More tweets" is vague. "20 tweets per week focused on [specific pain point]" is real.

### Question 3A: Underperforming Channel Review

**Re-ground:** Golden channel identified. Phase 3 is deciding which channels to kill.

**Simplify:** If a channel has high CAC + low retention + can't be scaled, stop doing it. Save time for what works.

**Recommend:** Which channels should you stop focusing on?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I can clearly identify 1-2 channels to kill
B) A few channels are weak but I'm not sure about killing
C) Hard to say without data

**After user identifies weak channels, ask:** What will you do instead with the time/budget? Reinvest in golden channel? Experiment with new channels?

## Phase 4: Design Experiments (1-2 New Channels)

### Question 4A: Untested Channel Opportunity

**Re-ground:** Bad channels killed. Phase 4 is: what's the next channel to try?

**Simplify:** You've proven some channels work. Now experiment with 1-2 new channels. Not spray-and-pray (running 10 channels at once). Pick one hypothesis and test it. "Our customers hang out on Reddit, so if we [specific tactic], we'll get [X users] at $[Y] CAC."

**Recommend:** What's one new channel your customers might be on that you haven't tried?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I have a specific channel in mind with a testable hypothesis
B) A few ideas but not a clear hypothesis yet
C) Not sure what to try
D) Let's keep focusing on golden channel only

**If A:** "Great. Tell me the hypothesis in this format: 'Our ideal customers [hang out at X], so if we [tactic], we'll acquire [N] users at $[Y] CAC in [timeframe]'"

---

### Question 4B: Experiment Design

**Re-ground:** New channel identified. Now: design the test.

**Simplify:** Pick success metrics (CAC <$X, D7 retention >Y%, timeline 2 weeks). If it doesn't hit those metrics, kill it. If it does, scale it.

**Recommend:** What would success look like for this experiment?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Target CAC, target retention, timeline all defined
B) Some targets but not all
C) "We'll try it and see what happens"
D) Just tell me how to set this up

**If C:** "That's spray-and-pray. We need clear targets so we know if it worked. Do you want to try this channel or wait?"

### Golden Channel Criteria

1. Lowest CAC among repeatable channels
2. Highest retention (users who actually stick around)
3. Scalability (can you do more of it?)
4. Alignment (users match your ideal customer profile)

**Examples:**

- **Twitter organic + word-of-mouth:** CAC $0, D7 retention 65%, scalable (requires founder time), limited ceiling
- **Google Ads:** CAC $18, D7 retention 35%, very high scalability (unlimited budget), needs product improvement
- **Strategic partnerships:** CAC $0-5, D7 retention 50%, high scalability (many partner targets), underexplored

## Red Flags to Avoid (Reference)

1. **"Our CAC is only $X because we got early adopters from [free channel]."**
   - Reality: Once you move to paid channels, costs rise. Paid CAC is the real benchmark.

2. **"We'll do content marketing and go viral."**
   - Reality: Content is slow. With 2-3 month runway, you need immediate channels. Content is for month 6+.

3. **"Let's do paid ads, it scales infinitely."**
   - Reality: Only if your product converts and retains. Bad product + paid ads = expensive fail.

4. **"We'll hire a growth person to fix this."**
   - Reality: Growth person optimizes. If you don't have a working channel to optimize, they're useless.

## Monthly Review Checklist (Reference)

Run this every month:

- [ ] Did channels perform as expected?
- [ ] Did any channel's CAC change? (More expensive = less attractive)
- [ ] Did retention stay consistent?
- [ ] Did new experiment show promise or kill it?
- [ ] Are we doubling down on golden channel or diversifying too much?
- [ ] What's the next channel to experiment with?

## What NOT to Do

**No spray and pray.** Don't:
- Run ads to every platform simultaneously (dilutes learning)
- Copy what competitors are doing without understanding your own data
- Hire growth person before you have repeatable channel (premature)
- Spend on vanity metrics (Twitter followers, newsletter opens) that don't translate to retention
