# Fundraise Prep

You're thinking about raising capital. Before you start pitching, prepare with brutal honesty. Investors can smell bullshit.

---

## Phase 0: Readiness Check

**Purpose:** Determine if you should raise NOW or build more traction first

**Exit criteria:** Clear go/no-go decision on fundraising timing

### What This Skill Does

You're going to assess whether you're ready to raise capital and, if you raise, what story you tell. This isn't about hype. It's about being so honest about your metrics that investors trust you. If your MRR is $800, say it. Then say: here's how we get to $10K.

**Key principle:** Investors fund momentum and team. If you don't have traction, the deck won't save you. If you do, honesty will accelerate the process. VCs respect founders who know their numbers and their gaps.

---

## Phase 1: Readiness Assessment (One Question at a Time)

### Question 1A: Product-Market Fit Signals

**Re-ground:** We're assessing if you should raise. Phase 1 is: do you have product-market fit signals?

**Simplify:** Product-market fit means: users love it so much they keep coming back and tell others. Not "we have traction." But "traction is growing AND sticky." Investors won't fund you without signals.

**Recommend:** Do you have these signals?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Yes: >30% D7 retention + users paying or heavily engaged + >0.3 viral coefficient
B) Some signals but not all (e.g., good retention but no revenue)
C) Early traction but no clear pattern yet
D) Not yet — we're pre-product-market fit

**If A:** "Good. You have a shot at raising. Let's prepare."

**If D:** "Don't raise yet. Use 6 months to prove traction first. Raising before product-market fit = expensive distraction. You'll spend 3 months pitching instead of shipping."

---

### Question 1B: TAM Reality Check

**Re-ground:** PMF signals assessed. Now: is your market big enough?

**Simplify:** VCs need >$100M market opportunity. If your TAM is $20M, most VCs won't take a meeting. But be honest about sizing. "$50B" for everything remotely related is BS.

**Recommend:** What's your honest TAM (Total Addressable Market)?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) TAM is >$100M and it's growing
B) TAM is $50-100M
C) TAM is $20-50M (small but viable)
D) TAM is <$20M or I haven't calculated it

**If B or C:** "OK, viable for early-stage but tough for VC. Will you bootstrap instead or focus on accelerating growth first?"

**If D:** "Calculate it now. Use a bottoms-up approach: # of potential customers × average price point. Don't make up numbers."

---

### Question 1C: Team Completeness

**Re-ground:** TAM assessed. Now: does your team look fundable?

**Simplify:** Do you have co-founders? Can you sell? Do you have technical talent? Missing any obvious gaps that investors will spot?

**Recommend:** Describe your founding team and what you each do.

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Multiple co-founders with complementary skills (technical, sales, product)
B) Solo founder with strong track record
C) Solo founder, no track record
D) Founder + early advisors but no true co-founder

**If C:** "That's tough. Most VCs won't fund solo founders. Can you recruit a co-founder before fundraising? Or have strong advisors who commit to working closely?"

---

### Question 1D: Runway and Timing

**Re-ground:** Team assessed. Now: do you have time to raise?

**Simplify:** Raising takes 3 months. If you have 2 months of runway, you're desperate and investors know it. If you have 18 months, you can be selective.

**Recommend:** How many months of runway do you have right now?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) 12+ months — I can be selective about raises
B) 6-12 months — I need funding but not desperate
C) 3-6 months — I need funding soon
D) <3 months — I'm in crisis mode

**If D:** "You're negotiating from weakness. Investors will lowball you. Can you bootstrap or raise from friends/angels faster to buy time?"

## Phase 2: Honest Traction Assessment

### Question 2A: Metrics Review

**Re-ground:** Readiness checked. Phase 2 is: what are your actual metrics?

**Simplify:** You need: Users, Growth rate, MRR, Retention, CAC. Be brutally honest. "We have 500 free users, 3 paying customers, $200 MRR" is better than spinning numbers.

**Recommend:** What are your key metrics right now?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I have all metrics organized and honest
B) I have most metrics but some are unclear
C) I'm not sure what metrics to report
D) I want to clean up the data before sharing

**If A:** "Good. Now, are these fundable numbers for seed stage?"

---

### Question 2B: Gap Analysis

**Re-ground:** Metrics stated. Now: how do they compare to investor benchmarks?

**Simplify:** Seed investors want: >$5K MRR OR >1K users with clear monetization path. If you have $200 MRR, you're below benchmark. That's OK if you can show clear path to $10K within 6 months.

**Recommend:** For each metric, how do you stack up?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I meet or exceed most seed benchmarks
B) I'm below some benchmarks but have a clear 6-month path to exceeding them
C) I'm below benchmarks and path is unclear
D) I'm significantly behind and need coaching on what's fundable

**If B:** "Good. Your story isn't 'we're already there.' It's 'here's where we are, here's where we'll be, here's how we get there.'"

---

## Phase 3: Build Pitch Deck Outline

### Question 3A: Problem Slide

**Re-ground:** Gaps analyzed. Phase 3 is: build the deck.

**Simplify:** Slide 1 is the problem. Not abstract. Paint a picture of customer's pain from a real customer interview. "Sales teams spend 8 hours a week on data entry. That's $50K of wasted time per person per year."

**Recommend:** Can you articulate the problem in one customer-centric sentence?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I have a clear problem statement from customer interviews
B) I know the problem but not articulated for investors yet
C) I have a vague sense of the problem
D) I'm building a feature for myself, not solving a market problem

**If D:** "Danger. 'I want to build X' doesn't fund. 'People have pain Y, I'm solving it' does. Do customer interviews first."

---

### Question 3B: Why Now Slide

**Re-ground:** Problem captured. Now: why is now the right time?

**Simplify:** What changed in the world that makes your solution possible NOW, not 2 years ago? New tech? Regulatory shift? Remote work? Don't say "mobile is the future" (obvious). Say "70% of sales teams work remote, existing tools don't support async collab."

**Recommend:** What's your "why now" evidence?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I have clear market timing evidence (new tech, regulatory shift, trend)
B) I can articulate it but may need to strengthen the evidence
C) I'm solving a timeless problem (hard to pitch)
D) I haven't thought about this

**If C:** "That's tougher for VC. You need SOME element of timing. Market shift? New technology? Demographic change? What's the timing angle?"

---

### Question 3C: Solution Slide

**Re-ground:** Why now captured. Now: show the solution.

**Simplify:** Don't show wireframes. Show actual product. 2-minute demo if possible. How does it solve the problem? Why is your approach better than alternatives?

**Recommend:** Do you have a polished product demo ready?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Yes, I have a compelling demo showing the product working
B) I have a working product but demo needs polish
C) I have a video walkthrough
D) Product is still early/rough — not ready to demo

**If D:** "You need to wait or show progress. 'We're still building' doesn't fund. Investors want to see traction, not promises."

---

### Question 3D: Traction Slide

**Re-ground:** Solution shown. Now: the only honest slide.

**Simplify:** Users, growth rate, revenue, retention. Show everything. No hiding bad metrics. Investors respect honesty and will ask about weaknesses anyway.

**Recommend:** Ready to show your traction metrics honestly?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Yes, I'm proud of my traction and can show it
B) Traction is mixed — some good, some weak
C) Traction is weak and I'm worried about investor reaction
D) I'm going to work on traction before pitching

**If C:** "Don't spin it. Say: 'We're early, here's what's working, here's our path to [benchmark]. This is why we need capital.'"

---

### Question 3E: Market Size Slide

**Re-ground:** Traction shown. Now: market size (realistic, not BS).

**Simplify:** TAM, SAM, SOM. TAM = everyone in the world who has this pain. SAM = people you can reach. SOM = realistic 5-year goal.

**Example:** "TAM is $50B, but we're going after the $500M mid-market SaaS segment. SOM in 5 years: $10M ARR."

**Recommend:** Can you articulate TAM, SAM, SOM?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Yes, with credible research backing
B) I have estimates but they're rough
C) I haven't broken this down
D) I have one big number but haven't segmented

**If D:** "Investors will want to understand what % of market you're going after, not the total addressable universe."

---

### Question 3F: Competition Slide

**Re-ground:** Market sized. Now: competition.

**Simplify:** Don't say "no competitors." You have them. Show the matrix: competitor name, their strength, their weakness, your advantage.

**Recommend:** Who are your direct competitors and how do you win?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I have clear competitive positioning and can explain our advantage
B) I can name competitors but advantage is fuzzy
C) Lots of indirect competitors, hard to isolate
D) I'm not sure who my competitors are

**If D:** "You're not doing customer research enough. Your users have alternatives (even if it's 'do it manually'). Who are they choosing over?"

---

### Question 3G: Business Model Slide

**Re-ground:** Competition captured. Now: how do you make money?

**Simplify:** Price per customer. How many customers to $1M ARR? Monthly/yearly contracts?

**Recommend:** What's your business model?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Clear pricing, clear path to $1M ARR
B) Pricing set but path to scale is fuzzy
C) Still figuring out monetization
D) Freemium or free-to-paid model

**If C:** "Investors want to see you've thought about this. Don't need final pricing, but show: potential customer × price point = revenue."

---

### Question 3H: Team Slide

**Re-ground:** Business model shown. Now: why you?

**Simplify:** What's your background? Why are you uniquely qualified to win? Sarah built payment infrastructure at Stripe, saw the problem firsthand. Mike scaled 3 growth teams. Combined = credible execution team.

**Recommend:** What's your founder story?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I have a strong background for this problem
B) I have relevant experience but may need to position it better
C) No direct experience but learning fast
D) Completely new to this space

**If D:** "That's a risk. Investors back teams that know their domain. Either find a co-founder with expertise or spend 6 months learning from users/competitors."

## Phase 4: Address Red Flags

### Question 4A: Red Flag Inventory

**Re-ground:** Deck outlined. Phase 4 is: what will investors worry about?

**Simplify:** Investors will ask about: growth slowing, retention low, no revenue, crowded market, incomplete team. Be ready with honest answers, not defensive ones.

**Recommend:** What are the 3 biggest red flags investors will see in your metrics?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I can name them and I have prepared answers
B) I can name them but my answers are weak
C) I'm worried about how investors will react
D) My metrics don't have obvious red flags

**If A:** "Perfect. We'll refine your answers."

**If C:** "Good instinct. Let's prepare responses that are honest, not defensive. 'Growth is slowing because we're optimizing for retention over CAC. Here's our path forward.'"

---

### Question 4B: Growth Slowing?

**Re-ground:** Red flags inventoried. Now: specific red flag responses.

**Simplify:** If Month 1: 100 users, Month 2: 150, Month 3: 160 → that looks like plateau. Your answer: "Early growth was early adopters. We're now optimizing for sustainable channels."

**Recommend:** Is your growth slowing? If so, why and what's your fix?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) Growth is accelerating — no problem
B) Growth is steady
C) Growth is slowing, and I have clear explanation and fix plan
D) Growth is slowing and I'm worried

**If D:** "Don't be. Slowing growth in month 3 is normal. Your story is: 'Here's why, here's the fix, here's the path forward.' That's fundable."

---

### Question 4C: Retention Red Flag?

**Re-ground:** Growth addressed. Now: retention.

**Simplify:** If D7 retention is <10%, that's a red flag. Investors think "product doesn't work." Your answer: "We identified [specific issue], fixing [feature], expect D7 to improve to 40% next month."

**Recommend:** What's your D7 retention and is it a concern?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) D7 retention is >30% — good
B) D7 retention is 10-30% — mentionable but not disqualifying
C) D7 retention is <10% — major red flag
D) D7 retention is strong but churn is concerning

---

### Question 4D: Revenue Red Flag?

**Re-ground:** Retention addressed. Now: revenue.

**Simplify:** If zero paying customers: "We're at 3K free users, 200 asked to pay early. Launching paid tier next month." That's fundable. "We're still validating business model" is not.

**Recommend:** What's your revenue story?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) We have paying customers and growing MRR
B) We're zero revenue but have clear path to monetization
C) We're zero revenue and still figuring it out
D) Revenue model is different (usage-based, freemium, etc.)

---

## Phase 5: Financial Model

### Question 5A: 3-Year Projection

**Re-ground:** Red flags addressed. Phase 5 is: financial modeling.

**Simplify:** Build 3-year projection: Users, MRR, expenses. Don't project hockey stick. Base it on actual growth rate (if growing 10% monthly, don't project 30% in month 12).

**Recommend:** Can you project 3 years of growth honestly?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I have a projection based on actual growth rate
B) I have rough estimates
C) I haven't done this yet
D) I'm worried my projections are too conservative

**If D:** "Good. Better conservative than delusional. Show your math: [current growth rate] × [months] = [conservative projection]."

---

### Question 5B: Path to Profitability

**Re-ground:** Projection made. Now: when do you break even?

**Simplify:** At your current burn and growth, when do you hit profitability? Or: how much capital do you need to get there?

**Recommend:** What's your path to profitability?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) We hit profitability in [X months] at current burn rate
B) We need funding to accelerate growth, then hit profitability
C) We're not profitable in 3 years at current burn
D) We're not sure

**If C:** "That's a problem for VC. You need to show path to profitability within 5-7 years or show unrealistic growth required to justify raise."

---

### Question 6A: Market Timing Evidence

**Re-ground:** Financials done. Phase 6 is: craft the why now narrative.

**Simplify:** Connect market timing to why you can WIN now. Not "mobile is the future." But "remote work doubled job market, recruiting tools are broken because they're designed for in-office."

**Recommend:** What's your "why now" story with timing evidence?

**Use AskUserQuestion to ask:**

**Options:**

A) I have clear timing evidence (tech trend, regulatory, market shift, demographic)
B) I can articulate it but weak on evidence
C) I'm solving a timeless problem (harder to pitch)
D) I haven't thought about this

## Pitch Deck Outline (Reference)

### Slide 1: The Problem (30 seconds)
- Paint a picture of the customer's pain
- Use a quote from customer interviews (real words)
- Show size of pain (how much time/money wasted? who feels it?)

Example: "Sales teams spend 8 hours a week on data entry. That's wasted time they could spend closing deals."

### Slide 2: Why Now? (30 seconds)
- What's changed in the world that makes this solvable now?
- Timing evidence: New tech, regulatory change, market shift, trend
- Don't say "mobile is the future" (everyone knows). Say "70% of sales teams now work remote, requiring new tools for async collab."

### Slide 3: The Solution (1 minute)
- Show actual product (not wireframe)
- How does it solve the problem?
- Why is your approach better than alternatives?

### Slide 4: Traction (2 minutes) — **This is your only honest slide**
- Users: [X]
- Growth rate: [X% monthly]
- Revenue: $[X] MRR
- Retention: [X% D7 / D30]
- Highlight: "3 paying customers, including [well-known company]"
- Don't hide bad metrics. Show everything.

### Slide 5: Market Size (30 seconds)
- TAM (Total Addressable Market): [$ size]
- SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): [$ size - what you can actually reach]
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): [$ size - realistic 5-year goal]

**Honest positioning:**
- "TAM is $50B, but we're going after $500M initial market (mid-market SaaS)"
- Better than "TAM is $200B" (no one believes it)

### Slide 6: Competition (1 minute)
- Who are your competitors? (Don't say "no competitors" — you have them)
- Why do you win?

| Competitor | Strength | Weakness | Your advantage |
|------------|----------|----------|----------------|
| [Competitor] | [What they do well] | [What they suck at] | [Why you're better] |

### Slide 7: Business Model (30 seconds)
- How do you make money?
- Price per customer: $[X]
- How many customers to $1M ARR? [X]

### Slide 8: Team (1 minute)
- Founder background: Why you? (What unique insight/advantage do you have?)
- Cofounders: Roles and why they're great
- Key hire: Who's next? (What's the gap?)

Example: "Sarah built payment infrastructure at Stripe, saw the problem firsthand. Mike scaled 3 growth teams. We're hiring [role] next to lead product."

### Slide 9: Ask (30 seconds)
- Raising: $[X]
- Use of funds: 18 months of runway
- Target: [Series A / Seed / Friends & family]

**Honest breakdown:**
- Salaries (3 people): $[X]
- Server/hosting: $[X]
- Marketing: $[X]
- Runway: [Months] at current burn

### Slide 10: Vision (30 seconds)
- Where's this going in 5 years?
- Why should this investor care?

Example: "In 5 years, every SaaS company uses our product. We'll be the infrastructure layer for team productivity, just like Stripe is for payments."

## Red Flag Responses (Reference)

### 1. "Your growth is slowing."

**If Month 1: 100 users, Month 2: 150 users, Month 3: 160 users**

**Your honest answer:**
- If true: "Initial growth was early adopters. We're now optimizing for sustainable channels. New strategy expected to reverse decline."
- Don't spin it.

### 2. "Your retention is terrible."

**If 30% D1 or <10% D7**

**Your honest answer:**
- "We're fixing [specific issue]. Here's what we learned from churn interviews. We're rebuilding [feature] and expect D7 to improve to 40% next month."

### 3. "You have no revenue."

**If zero paying customers**

**Your honest answer:**
- If B2C: "We're at 3K free users, preparing paid tier. We've validated value — 200 users asked to pay early."
- If B2B: "We have 5 pilot customers. 2 will sign contracts next month. CAC will be $[X]."

### 4. "You're in a crowded market with big competitors."

**Your honest answer:**
- "Yes, and here's why we win: [specific differentiation]. We're targeting [underserved segment] that [big competitor] ignores."

### 5. "Your team is incomplete."

**If you're solo founder or missing critical skills**

**Your honest answer:**
- "I've committed [cofounder name] who brings [skill]. Hiring [role] this month. Here's why we'll execute despite the gap."
