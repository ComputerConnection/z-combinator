# Fundraise Prep

You're thinking about raising capital. Before you start pitching, prepare with brutal honesty. Investors can smell bullshit.

## What You Do

1. Read METRICS-REPORT.md to understand your actual traction
2. Read LEAN-CANVAS.md for market context
3. Build a pitch deck outline grounded in real data
4. Identify key metrics investors will ask about
5. Assess gaps honestly: "Your MRR is $800. Most VCs won't take a meeting. Here's your path to $10K/month."
6. Prepare competitive landscape with real comparisons
7. Draft financial model (hiring, burn rate, revenue projection)
8. Build "why now" narrative with market timing evidence
9. Identify red flags investors will spot and prepare honest responses

## Before You Raise: Reality Check

**Ask yourself honestly:**

1. **Do you have product-market fit signals?**
   - >30% D7 retention?
   - Users paying or engaged heavily?
   - Natural organic growth (referral rate >0.3)?
   - If no: Raise is premature. Use this 6 months to prove traction first.

2. **What problem are you solving?**
   - TAM >$100M? (If smaller, most VCs won't be interested)
   - Is it growing? (Market timing)
   - Can your startup uniquely win? (Defensible moat or team advantage)

3. **Do you have the right team?**
   - Can the founder sell (raise, land customers, recruit)?
   - Do you have technical cofounders or hire strong engineers immediately?
   - Are there obvious gaps in the team that VCs will question?

4. **What's your honest runway?**
   - Months of burn left: [X]
   - Will raising be a distraction (3 months of pitching)?
   - Can you ship while raising? (Traction doesn't stop during fundraise)

## Pitch Deck Outline

### Slide 1: The Problem (30 seconds)
- Paint a picture of the customer's pain
- Use a quote from customer interviews (real words)
- Show size of pain (how much time/money wasted? who feels it?)

Example: "Sales teams spend 8 hours a week on data entry. That's wasted time they could spend closing deals."

### Slide 2: Why Now? (30 seconds)
- What's changed in the world that makes this solvable now?
- Timing evidence: New tech, regulatory change, market shift, trend
- Don't say "mobile is the future" (everyone knows). Say "70% of sales teams now work remote, requiring new tools for async collab."

### Slide 3: The Solution (1 minute)
- Show actual product (not wireframe)
- How does it solve the problem?
- Why is your approach better than alternatives?

### Slide 4: Traction (2 minutes) — **This is your only honest slide**
- Users: [X]
- Growth rate: [X% monthly]
- Revenue: $[X] MRR
- Retention: [X% D7 / D30]
- Highlight: "3 paying customers, including [well-known company]"
- Don't hide bad metrics. Show everything.

### Slide 5: Market Size (30 seconds)
- TAM (Total Addressable Market): [$ size]
- SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): [$ size - what you can actually reach]
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): [$ size - realistic 5-year goal]

**Honest positioning:**
- "TAM is $50B, but we're going after $500M initial market (mid-market SaaS)"
- Better than "TAM is $200B" (no one believes it)

### Slide 6: Competition (1 minute)
- Who are your competitors? (Don't say "no competitors" — you have them)
- Why do you win?

| Competitor | Strength | Weakness | Your advantage |
|------------|----------|----------|----------------|
| [Competitor] | [What they do well] | [What they suck at] | [Why you're better] |

Example:
| Competitor | Strength | Weakness | Your advantage |
|------------|----------|----------|----------------|
| Salesforce | All-in-one CRM | $150/user/month, slow | $50/user/month, 10x faster setup |
| HubSpot | Easy setup | Only for SMB, lacks custom fields | SMB-to-enterprise scaling |
| Our solution | Fast + affordable | Early product | Founder understood the pain |

### Slide 7: Business Model (30 seconds)
- How do you make money?
- Price per customer: $[X]
- How many customers to $1M ARR? [X]

### Slide 8: Team (1 minute)
- Founder background: Why you? (What unique insight/advantage do you have?)
- Cofounders: Roles and why they're great
- Key hire: Who's next? (What's the gap?)

Example: "Sarah built payment infrastructure at Stripe, saw the problem firsthand. Mike scaled 3 growth teams. We're hiring [role] next to lead product."

### Slide 9: Ask (30 seconds)
- Raising: $[X]
- Use of funds: 18 months of runway
- Target: [Series A / Seed / Friends & family]

**Honest breakdown:**
- Salaries (3 people): $[X]
- Server/hosting: $[X]
- Marketing: $[X]
- Runway: [Months] at current burn

### Slide 10: Vision (30 seconds)
- Where's this going in 5 years?
- Why should this investor care?

Example: "In 5 years, every SaaS company uses our product. We'll be the infrastructure layer for team productivity, just like Stripe is for payments."

## Key Metrics Investors Will Ask About

Prepare answers to these:

| Metric | You said | Reality | Concern | Your answer |
|--------|----------|---------|---------|-------------|
| Users | [X] | [Breakdown: paid vs free, active vs inactive] | Inflated numbers? | [Honest breakdown] |
| Growth rate | [X% monthly] | [Sustainable? Declining?] | Is it slowing? | [Momentum assessment] |
| CAC | $[X] | [By channel] | Unsustainable? | [Path to unit economics] |
| LTV | $[X] | [Based on what assumptions?] | Do users actually pay? | [Revenue truth] |
| Churn | [X%] | [How do you measure?] | Product not sticky? | [Retention improvement plan] |
| Runway | [X months] | [With what burn rate?] | When do you run out of money? | [Explicit timeline] |

## Red Flags Investors Will Spot

Be ready for these questions:

### 1. "Your growth is slowing."
- If Month 1: 100 users, Month 2: 150 users, Month 3: 160 users
- Investor reads this as "plateau, not product-market fit"

**Your honest answer:**
- If true: "Initial growth was early adopters. We're now optimizing for sustainable channels. New strategy expected to reverse decline."
- Don't spin it.

### 2. "Your retention is terrible."
- If 30% D1 or <10% D7
- Investor reads this as "product doesn't work for most users"

**Your honest answer:**
- "We're fixing [specific issue]. Here's what we learned from churn interviews. We're rebuilding [feature] and expect D7 to improve to 40% next month."

### 3. "You have no revenue."
- If zero paying customers
- Investor reads this as "unproven business model"

**Your honest answer:**
- If B2C: "We're at 3K free users, preparing paid tier. We've validated value — 200 users asked to pay early."
- If B2B: "We have 5 pilot customers. 2 will sign contracts next month. CAC will be $[X]."

### 4. "You're in a crowded market with big competitors."
- Investor reads this as "commodity, will be crushed"

**Your honest answer:**
- "Yes, and here's why we win: [specific differentiation]. We're targeting [underserved segment] that [big competitor] ignores."

### 5. "Your team is incomplete."
- If you're solo founder or missing critical skills
- Investor reads this as "execution risk"

**Your honest answer:**
- "I've committed [cofounder name] who brings [skill]. Hiring [role] this month. Here's why we'll execute despite the gap."

## Financial Model

Build a simple 3-year projection:

```
Year 1 (Months 1-12)
- Users: Start [X], End [X]
- MRR growth: Month 1: $[X], Month 12: $[X]
- Expenses: [Salary] + [Infrastructure] + [Marketing] = $[X]/month
- Runway consumed: [X months] of raise

Year 2 (Months 13-24)
- Users: Start [X], End [X]
- MRR: [Realistic projection]
- Path to profitability: [Do you get there?]

Year 3 (Months 25-36)
- Users: [X]
- ARR: $[X]
- Profitability: [Yes/approaching/not yet]
```

**Honest modeling:**
- Don't project hockey stick growth
- Base projections on actual growth rate (don't assume it accelerates 10x)
- Include salary for yourself (don't pretend to work for free)

## "Why Now" Narrative

Connect market timing to why you can win now.

Examples:
- "AI made this 10x cheaper to build. Before, only Uber could afford this problem. Now any startup can."
- "Remote work doubled job market. Recruiting is broken. This is the first tool built for remote teams."
- "Crypto enables new business models. Payments without intermediaries. That wasn't possible 2 years ago."

## Investor Red Flags (Things that make you unfundable)

- No cofounder (investor wants proof you can work with people)
- Team doesn't match the problem (no technical founder for technical product)
- Asking for money without traction AND without a clear unfair advantage
- Unwilling to take feedback (aggressive defensiveness = red flag for investor management)
- No clear exit path (investor wants to know how they get their money back)
